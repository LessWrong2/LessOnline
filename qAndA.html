Whose writings is this event about?

This event is for those who write fiction or non-fiction inspired (in part) by an aspiration to rationality.

  • Blogs like Astral Codex Ten, Don't Worry About the Vase, PutANumOnIt, Minding Our Way, Gwern, and more, along with the community blog LessWrong.
  • Stories like Harry Potter and the Methods of Rationality, UNSONG, Worth the Candle, Luminosity, Planecrash, and more.

What sorts of things will happen at LessOnline?

  • Double Cruxes: Pairs of people with substantive disagreements will work together to find the crux of their disagreements â€” a subquestion that would change both of their minds. Pairs include Eli Tyre & Anna Salamon, Oliver Habryka & Ben Hoffman, and more.
  • Storytelling: Authors will read new stories that they have written, including Alexander Wales, Scott Alexander, Miranda Dixon-Luinenberg, and more.
  • Podcasts: Some typical rational podcasts will have a live recording, including The Bayesian Conspiracy, Minds Almost Meeting, and The Filan Cabinet.
  • Workshops: Some former CFAR Instructors (e.g. Duncan Sabien, Eli Tyre) will give classes on tools like Murphyjitsu and Trigger-Action Plans, and Ricki Heicklen will lead a day-long Trading School she developed while working at Jane Street.
  • Singalong: Local Bard Raymond Arnold will lead some group singalongs.
  • Memo Sessions: Writers will hand out printed copies of draft blogposts for small group discussions with the writer, including Katja Grace, Dominic Cummings, John Wentworth, Kaj Sotala, and more.
