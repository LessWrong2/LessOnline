What is LessOnline?

31 May - 2 June, 2024

Lighthaven Campus, Berkeley, CA

LessOnline is an in-person conference for the cluster of writers closely associated with the rationalist community, an opportunity to meet people you've only ever known by their LessWrong username or pseudonymous Substack handle.

The goal is to bring together a "mostly-online subculture of people trying to work together to figure out how to distinguish truth from falsehood using insights from probability theory, cognitive science, and AI." [1] Astral Codex Ten

The structure will be determined by you: We'll have a collaborative schedule for "unconference" sessions, where attendees can sign up to run anything they want. In addition, writers highlighted in green will be contributing sessions at some point over the weekend.

A note from Ben Pace

I don't claim that every blogger invited actively identifies with the rationalist flag, but I confidently claim that there is a natural clustering of blogs here that distinguishes them from the 99%+ of other blogs in the world that don't fit into this clustering, and I know of no other name where everyone else also knows what it refers to.

You can also consider it the list of blogs that semi- regularly links to and has maybe written at least one critical response to SlateStarCodex/AstralCodexTen, which I think helps define the cluster, but I don't think the cluster is about SSC/ACX.

I think there's a common thread to a lot of the writers invited:

  • A belief in the ability to break the world down into parts and understand how it works.
  • A more specific belief in the ability to do this to our own minds and decision-making algorithms (as well as the ability to improve them).
  • A belief that successfully explaining how the world works is a powerful step for changing how the world works.

These sites embody the virtues we want to celebrate. We've sent free tickets to everyone whose site is listed below.

What is Lighthaven?

Lighthaven is a space dedicated to hosting educational events and programs that help people think better and to improve humanity's long-term trajectory.

Past programs we hosted and were excited about have been the MATS program, which helps young researchers break into the field of AI Alignment and workshops run by the Machine Intelligence Research Institute and the Topos institute on helping people better understand the long-term consequences of developing Artificial General Intelligence.

Lighthaven is a project by Lightcone Infrastructure, which is dedicated to facilitating intellectual progress on humanity's most important questions.
